What Each Table Does

Customer Core

Stores everyone who has placed or may place an order. Since the store runs on Instagram, the instagram_handle field is essential for communication.

  • PK customer_id
  • full_name
  • phone
  • email
  • instagram_handle
  • address
  • created_at
Product Core

The master catalog entry for any item sold. Holds fields common to both thrift and handmade items. Product-type-specific details live in sub-tables.

  • PK product_id
  • name
  • description
  • product_type (thrift / handmade)
  • price
  • category
  • created_at
Thrift_Item Sub-type

Extends Product with details specific to second-hand items. The is_sold boolean is critical — thrift pieces are unique; once sold, the item is gone.

  • PK thrift_item_id
  • FK product_id
  • condition
  • size
  • color
  • brand
  • is_sold
Handmade_Item Sub-type

Extends Product for items the creator makes in batches. Tracks quantity_available — unlike thrift items, multiple units can exist.

  • PK handmade_item_id
  • FK product_id
  • size
  • color
  • material
  • quantity_available
Order Transaction

Represents a single purchase event by a customer. Tracks when it was placed, overall status, and total amount.

  • PK order_id
  • FK customer_id
  • order_date
  • order_status
  • total_amount
  • notes
Order_Item Junction

The bridge table linking Orders to Products. Stores which products were in which order, quantity, and price at time of purchase.

  • PK order_item_id
  • FK order_id
  • FK product_id
  • quantity
  • unit_price
Payment Financial

Tracks payment for a specific order. Records method, status, and a reference ID for reconciliation.

  • PK payment_id
  • FK order_id
  • amount_paid
  • payment_method
  • payment_status
  • transaction_ref
  • paid_at
Shipping Logistics

Tracks where the order is going and its delivery status. Records courier, tracking number, and timestamps for dispatch and delivery.

  • PK shipping_id
  • FK order_id
  • recipient_name
  • shipping_address
  • shipping_status
  • courier
  • tracking_number
  • shipped_at
  • delivered_at

How This Diagram Answers the Real Questions

1

What products are being sold?

The PRODUCT table is the central catalog. Every item has a row here with its name, description, category, and price.

2

Are they thrifted items or handmade items?

PRODUCT.product_type flags each item as "thrift" or "handmade". Beyond that, two sub-type tables — THRIFT_ITEM and HANDMADE_ITEM — each linked to a product row via FK, hold all type-specific details cleanly and separately.

3

How many pieces are available?

For handmade items: HANDMADE_ITEM.quantity_available tracks stock count. For thrift items: THRIFT_ITEM.is_sold is a boolean — thrift pieces are unique, so availability is either true or false.

4

Which customer placed which order?

ORDER.customer_id is a foreign key pointing to the CUSTOMER table. Joining these two on that field tells you exactly who placed any order, including their name and Instagram handle.

5

What all items were part of one order?

The ORDER_ITEM junction table links orders to products. Each row holds one order_id and one product_id. Filter by order ID to get all products in that order. unit_price is stored per row, so the price at time of purchase is preserved even if the product price changes later.

6

Was the order paid for?

PAYMENT.payment_status answers this directly — values like pending, paid, or failed. The table also records method (UPI, bank transfer, cash, etc.) and a transaction reference for verification.

7

Has the order been shipped or delivered?

SHIPPING.shipping_status tracks the stage — preparing, shipped, out for delivery, delivered. shipped_at and delivered_at give exact timestamps. Payment and shipping are kept as separate tables so each can be updated independently.

8

Can one customer place multiple orders?

Yes. The relationship between CUSTOMER and ORDER is one-to-many (||--o{). One customer record can link to many order records through ORDER.customer_id.

9

Can one order contain multiple products?

Yes. This is a many-to-many relationship between ORDER and PRODUCT, resolved by the ORDER_ITEM junction table. One order maps to many ORDER_ITEM rows; one product can appear across many orders.

10

How do we store product-specific details like size, category, color, condition, or price?

Fields common to all products (price, category, name) live in PRODUCT.
Thrift-only details (condition, brand, is_sold) live in THRIFT_ITEM.
Handmade-only details (material, quantity_available) live in HANDMADE_ITEM.

This split avoids null columns — a handmade item would leave condition and is_sold empty if everything were in one table, which is poor database design.
